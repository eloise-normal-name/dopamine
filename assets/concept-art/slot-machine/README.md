## Brief 03: Machine Component Close-ups

### Emotional Target
**"Precision meets personality"** â€” The machine should feel like a premium object. Materials communicate quality â€” brushed metal catches light, glass has depth, buttons have satisfying weight. Technical details should feel beautiful, not clinical.

### What to Depict
A series of close-up studies:

1. **The Reel Window** â€” The central viewport where symbols appear. Slightly rounded glass with subtle reflections, warm backlighting, a sense of depth beyond the surface.

2. **The Cabinet Frame** â€” Overall machine form. Art deco or retro-modern curves, chrome or brushed metal accents, neon edge lighting.

3. **Controls & Buttons** â€” Physical interaction points. Buttons with satisfying shape and material, displays that glow with warmth, levers or controls with mechanical character.

4. **Material Studies** â€” Close crops of surfaces: metal grain, glass transparency, glowing edge details, neon tube cross-sections.

### Composition
- **Tight framing**: Fill the frame with the subject
- **Dramatic lighting**: Neon edge-lighting reveals form and material
- **Selective focus**: Sharp subject, softly glowing background

### Technical Specs
- **Dimensions**: Various (1:1 for studies, 4:3 for component views)
- **Key materials**: Brushed metal (silver-blue), warm glass, chrome, glowing neon tube
- **Color treatment**: Purple-blue ambient with warm accent lighting
- **Detail level**: High enough to extract individual elements for sprites

### Reference Context
- High-end audio equipment photography (materials, craft, premium feel)
- Art deco architecture details (curves, chrome, geometric patterns)
- Pinball machine close-ups (glass, metal, light interaction)
- Mechanical watch photography (precision, beauty in engineering)
